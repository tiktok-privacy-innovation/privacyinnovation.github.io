Rules and Regulations

1. Introduction & Competition Overview

Welcome to the inaugural PET ARENA CTF COMPETITION, sponsored by TikTok Inc., a hands-on Capture the Flag (CTF) competition with the objective of breaking privacy-enhancing technologies (PETs) and protection mechanisms (e.g., differential privacy) with a mix of techniques, including machine learning, in an interactive database setting.

Participants will gain access to a simulated Database System (DBS) with static and evolving datasets through a dedicated SQL query client. Assuming the role of a malicious analyst, teams will design and execute novel or known privacy attacks across six missions organized into three tracks. The tracks and missions are of various difficulty levels and invite participants to perform database membership inference, infer sensitive attributes, reconstruct sensitive user records, link records across tables, and devise other innovative attacks in a final open mission.

A panel of distinguished cybersecurity and privacy experts will judge the innovative approach, technical soundness, and real-world impact of the submitted attacks.

This document outlines the official rules, regulations, and procedures for all participants. By submitting an application, all participants agree to be bound by these rules.

3. Eligibility and Team Formation

3.1. Team Composition

Teams must consist of a minimum of one (1) and a maximum of four (4) members. We invite any privacy, security, machine learning, and/or data expert, researcher, and engineer to participate. We especially encourage undergraduate or graduate students in these or related fields.

3.2. Team Lead

Each team must designate one member as the "Team Lead." The Team Lead will be the primary point of contact for all official communications from the competition organizers.

3.3. Global Participation

The competition is open to participants from anywhere in the world, unless prohibited by local law or U.S. export control regulations.

3.4. Conflicts of Interest

Employees, contractors, and immediate family members of the organizing committee, judging panel, and sponsoring organizations are ineligible to participate for monetary prizes but may participate unofficially.

4. Application and Vetting Process

4.1. Application Submission

All prospective teams must submit a single application via the official application link. Applications will be reviewed on a rolling basis.

4.2. Required Information

The application must include: Team Name, a designated Team Lead, full names, affiliations, and roles of all team members, a valid contact email address for the Team Lead, a brief team biography outlining individual expertise, and (optional) links to code samples or prior work.

4.3. Vetting

The organizing committee will vet all applications to ensure compliance with eligibility requirements. The committee reserves the right to approve or deny any application at its sole discretion. Successful applicants will receive credentials to access the competition environment.

5. Competition Structure and Objectives

5.1. Environment

Participants will interact with the target databases exclusively through designated APIs. Direct access to the underlying infrastructure is prohibited.

5.2. Tracks and Missions

The competition is divided into three tracks, containing a total of six missions.

5.3. Objective

The primary goal is to successfully compromise the privacy of the data protected by various mechanisms. Success is measured by the criteria outlined in Section 6.

6. Scoring and Judging Criteria

6.1. Judging Panel

A panel of independent experts will evaluate all submissions.

6.2. Scoring Matrix

Success is measured by a comprehensive scoring matrix. For most missions, scoring is objective. The "Open Mission" involves subjective judgment on innovation and soundness.

6.3. Evaluation Criteria

Submissions will be judged based on: Type of Attack and Privacy Compromise, Attack Metrics, Attack Detectability, Attack Complexity, Attacker's Strength and Prior Knowledge, and Attack Robustness.

6.4. Write-ups

For each mission, teams must submit a concise write-up detailing their methodology, code, and results. For the Open Mission, a detailed report is required. The quality of the write-up will be a factor in judging.

7. Prizes and Recognition

7.1. Monetary Awards

Prizes will be awarded to the top five teams as follows: 1st Place: U$10,000; 2nd Place: U$5,000; 3rd Place: U$2,000; 4th Place: U$1,000; 5th Place: U$500.

7.2. Additional Recognition

Honorable Mentions will be awarded for exceptional creativity. Certificates of Completion will be awarded to all who successfully complete at least one mission.

7.3. Award Ceremony

Winners will be celebrated at the hybrid award ceremony on November 15, 2025. Unfortunately, the organizing committee can not cover costs related to travel.

7.4. Taxes

All prize winners are solely responsible for any and all applicable federal, state, and local taxes.

8. Code of Conduct and Fair Play

8.1. Scope of Engagement

All activities must be strictly confined to the provided competition environment. Attacking the competition infrastructure is strictly prohibited.

8.2. Prohibited Actions

Denial of Service (DoS) attacks, sharing flags or solutions, disrupting the platform, or accessing other teams' data will result in immediate disqualification.

8.3. Professional Conduct

All participants must communicate respectfully. Harassment or abusive language will not be tolerated.

8.4. Final Authority

The organizing committee has the final authority to interpret these rules and to disqualify any team for violations.

9. Privacy Policy and Data Handling

By applying, you consent to the collection of personal data for vetting, administration, and prize distribution. All personal data will be treated as confidential and securely deleted within 12 months of the competition's conclusion, except for the names and affiliations of winning teams for archival purposes.

10. Legal Disclaimers & General Conditions

10.1. Intellectual Property

Participants retain IP rights to their novel methodologies. However, by participating, you grant the organizers a non-exclusive, royalty-free, worldwide license to use and publish your submission write-ups and results for academic and promotional purposes, with due credit.

10.2. Right to Modify

The organizers reserve the right to modify the rules or schedule. Changes will be communicated to all registered teams.

10.3. Liability

The organizers are not responsible for technical issues that may prevent participation.
